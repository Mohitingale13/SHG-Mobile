# SHG-Mobile Reports Catalog

The SHG-Mobile platform generates 13 distinct, mathematically rigorous PDF reports to ensure 100% transparency, compliance with Bank/NGO standards, and ease of auditing. All reports are dynamically generated on the device and can be downloaded in the user's preferred language (e.g., English or Marathi).

Here is the complete breakdown of all 13 reports, what they contain, and their purpose:

---

### 1. Savings Report / Collection Report
**Purpose:** Tracks all monthly member contributions, savings deposits, and any late fees collected during a specific period.
**What it does:** 
- Lists every member who made a payment within the selected date range.
- Details the date of payment, receipt number, amount paid, late fees, and whether it was paid via Cash or Online.
- Provides a summary of Total Collected, Total Late Fees, and the total count of Members who paid.

### 2. Member Passbook (Individual Ledger)
**Purpose:** A comprehensive, personalized financial ledger for a single specific member.
**What it does:** 
- Displays the member's complete financial history with the SHG.
- Includes total accumulated savings, active internal loans, and their share of any bank loans.
- Shows a chronological ledger of every single transaction (deposits, loan disbursements, EMI repayments) the member has made, with running balances for complete transparency.

### 3. Financial Report (Executive Summary)
**Purpose:** Provides a high-level overview of the SHG's overall financial health and cash flow.
**What it does:**
- Summarizes Total Group Savings, Total Cash In Bank/Hand, Outstanding Loans, and Total Interest Earned.
- Used heavily by the President/Treasurer to get a quick snapshot of the group's net worth and liquidity without digging through granular transaction logs.

### 4. Internal Loan Register
**Purpose:** The official register tracking all loans given from the SHG's internal funds to its members.
**What it does:**
- Lists all active, completed, or overdue internal loans.
- Displays the Member Name, Loan Amount, Interest Rate, Duration, Total Repaid, Remaining Balance, and Loan Status.
- Essential for auditing the internal capital distribution and ensuring members are repaying their EMIs.

### 5. Bank Loan Register
**Purpose:** Tracks large macro-loans taken by the SHG from an external Bank, and how that loan was sub-allocated to individual members.
**What it does:**
- Shows the total overarching Bank Loan amount and the bank's interest rate.
- Details exactly which members received a cut of that bank loan (allocations).
- Tracks the repayment progress of those specific members towards the bank loan, ensuring the SHG doesn't default on its external obligations.

### 6. Loan Recovery / Demand Report
**Purpose:** A forward-looking operational report used to track who owes money.
**What it does:**
- Calculates the EMI demands for the upcoming/current month.
- Highlights members who are overdue on their loan payments, showing exactly how much Principal and Interest they owe.
- Used by the Treasurer during meetings to demand payments and calculate recovery percentages.

### 7. Member Register
**Purpose:** The official KYC (Know Your Customer) and demographic register for the group.
**What it does:**
- Lists every member in the SHG, their assigned Roles (President, Treasurer, Secretary, Member), and their Join Dates.
- Used for official group documentation when opening bank accounts or applying for government schemes.

### 8. Annual Report (Balance Sheet)
**Purpose:** The definitive end-of-year financial statement.
**What it does:**
- Aggregates all financial data across the entire fiscal year.
- Acts as the official Balance Sheet and Profit/Loss statement for the SHG.
- Required by Banks, NGOs, and Government Auditors to assess the SHG's grading and eligibility for larger loans.

### 9. Meeting Register (Minutes Book)
**Purpose:** Digitizes the physical "Proccedings Book" or Meeting Minutes.
**What it does:**
- Documents the date, time, and attendance of every SHG meeting.
- Records all official resolutions passed during the meeting (e.g., "Approved Rs. 50,000 loan for Sunita").
- Ensures a legally binding digital paper trail of the group's democratic decisions.

### 10. Cash Book (Jama-Kharch Pustak)
**Purpose:** The day-to-day accounting ledger for physical cash handling.
**What it does:**
- Logs every single Cash-In (Income) and Cash-Out (Expense/Disbursement) transaction chronologically.
- Calculates the running "Cash in Hand" balance.
- If the Treasurer collects cash, it appears here. If they give a cash loan, it deducts from here. Prevents embezzlement.

### 11. Bank Book
**Purpose:** The day-to-day accounting ledger for the SHG's official Bank Account.
**What it does:**
- Mirrors the SHG's actual bank passbook.
- Tracks all digital/online payments, bank deposits made by the Treasurer, and bank withdrawals/cheques.
- Calculates the running "Cash in Bank" balance, allowing the group to cross-verify the app's math with their actual Bank Statement.

### 12. Internal Loan Statement (Passbook)
**Purpose:** A dedicated statement generated for a specific active internal loan.
**What it does:**
- Downloaded directly from the individual Loan Details screen.
- Summarizes the loan terms (principal, interest, duration) and shows a complete chronological ledger of every EMI paid towards this specific loan.
- Shows the breakdown of Principal vs Interest for each payment and the running outstanding balances.

### 13. Bank Loan Allocation Statement
**Purpose:** A dedicated statement for a member's specific chunk (allocation) of a group Bank Loan.
**What it does:**
- Downloaded directly from the Bank Loan Allocation details screen.
- Details the member's specific allocated principal, the bank's interest rate, and a chronological ledger of their repayments specifically targeting the bank loan.
- Crucial for members to track their individual liability towards the group's external debt.
