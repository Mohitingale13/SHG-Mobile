# SHG Onboarding & Operations Guide

This document outlines how the application handles the lifecycle of Self-Help Groups (SHGs), ensuring a smooth experience for both brand-new groups starting from scratch and established groups migrating from traditional paper ledgers.

---

## 1. New SHGs (Starting Fresh)
For groups that are just forming, the app acts as their first and only financial ledger. 

### A. Initial Setup
- **Registration:** The President creates the SHG on the app and receives a unique Group Code/Link.
- **Zero Balance:** The group naturally starts with an opening cash balance of **Rs. 0**.
- **Member Onboarding:** The President shares the invite link via WhatsApp. Members click the link, download the app, and instantly join the group.

### B. Daily Operations
- **Monthly Dues:** Starting from their very first month, the Treasurer logs the regular monthly contributions via the **Payments** tab.
- **Loans:** As the group builds capital over the first few months, they can begin issuing Internal Loans. The app tracks the EMI, Principal, and Interest automatically.
- **Reporting:** Because every single Rupee is tracked digitally from Day 1, the monthly and yearly PDF reports generated by the app will organically reflect a perfect financial history.

---

## 2. Existing SHGs (Migrating from Paper)
For groups that have been operating for years on paper, forcing them to manually enter thousands of past monthly payments for every member is unreasonable. The app uses a hybrid "Lump-Sum + Backdating" approach.

### A. The "Lump-Sum" Migration
During the initial app setup, the President/Treasurer is asked to input two critical numbers from their physical ledger:
1. **Total Accumulated Savings:** The total wealth the group has saved over the years.
2. **Current Cash/Bank Balance:** The actual liquid cash they have right now.
*This instantly synchronizes the app's core ledger with the physical books without needing years of historical data entry.*

### B. Migrating Active Operations (Backdating)
While old, completed transactions are summarized in the lump-sum, the group still needs to track *ongoing* obligations:
- **Outstanding Member Dues:** If a member missed their payment last month, the Treasurer can go to the **Payments** tab, click **Record Payment**, and use the dropdown menus to select the specific past Month/Year (e.g., "February 2024"). This accurately backdates the entry.
- **Active Internal Loans:** The Treasurer can recreate any active loans currently held by members. They can define the remaining principal and outstanding interest so the app takes over the EMI calculations moving forward.
- **Active Bank Loans:** Similarly, the group's overarching bank loan can be entered so the app tracks the remaining EMIs owed to the bank.

### C. Daily Operations & Auditing
- **Seamless Transition:** Once the opening balances and active loans are migrated, the group operates exactly like a new SHG. The dashboard will remind members of their *current* month's dues.
- **Fraud-Proof Auditing:** If a Treasurer records a backdated payment, the app saves the true digital timestamp of when the button was pressed. 
- **Flawless Reports:** The PDF reporting engine mathematically combines the "Lump-Sum Opening Balance" with any newly backdated entries and all future payments. This guarantees that the final Balance Sheet presented to Banks or NGOs is 100% accurate and mathematically sound.
